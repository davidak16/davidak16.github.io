Executive Summary

Summary of Figmas Current Dispostions

Figma brings something new with creative tools now available on the internet. Figma is offering a premium product that can be utilized by a myriad of different users. Figma's story starts back in 2011 when founders Dylan Field and Evan Wallace had a lightbulb. The initial idea came when they posed the question ‚ÄúCan tools for designers be built on the web? ‚ÄúNo need for final versions, people across the world and different time zones could collaborate and of course it would give the average person access to powerful design tools. Before they knew it an experiment in WebGLs proved Figmas validity and led to three more years of intensive work on a vector based design tool.

In 2015 Figma was born and it was free. Though it took a rocky start, the pros of Figma soon became clear to designers everywhere. Real time collaboration was one of the biggest starters. Additionally, Figma made design accessible to everyone. Companies began adopting Figma to speed up their workflows and it finally allowed people to work together outside work.

SWOT Analysis

Analysis of the Strengths, Weaknesses, Oppurtunities and Threats

  • STRENGTHS

    Figma allows you to easily create website mockups. One of the more obvious advantages Figma is that you don't need to know how to code. You can build a prototype by simply dragging and dropping objects onto the canvas . It has a Simple Interface unlike Photoshop which is difficult to navigate through the interface. Figma is much easier and only requires one click to add an object. It also has responsive designs which allows the program to automatically adjust the layout according to the size of the screen.

  • WEAKNESSES

    Some of the weaknesses found among users have been no version control this makes tracking all of the changes fairy difficult .Another people have noted is limited customization options, while customization is available to an extent people would like to see custom shapes, gradients, or patterns.No offline work is big while the novelty of Figma lies in it being desktop and cloud based offline work still is an important feature to consider.

  • OPPURTUNITIES

    The biggest opportunities for Figma are its Collaboration platform in FigJam and Its Education programs. Since Figma is cloud based, working together with others by sharing your files online is a natural progression and key point of interest. In the future many users have cited a work offline mode Figma currently relies on a internet connection like most desktop apps, compared to some competitors.

  • THREATS

    Right now Figma is poised ahead of many of its competitors . In large art due to the adobe acquisition. However it still directly competes with a lot of apps in the adobe suite. Current threats are similar cloud based platforms like Sketch and Canva, but Figma seems the most robust and is accepted as the industry standard in terms of enterprise software.

Competitive Analysis

A Comparative Analysis of Figma vs its Direct Competitors

Sketch,Canva,etc

Figma has the option to prototype and collaboration in case you are working in a team. It's been specifically made for this use case. Whereas canva and sketch seems to be a more general tool with the former being used more for social media graphics. Figma is definitely the all in one industry standard.

Design Apps

Sketch and Figma support pages and artboards, although Figma's artboards (or frames) are more flexible because they can be nested. Adobe XD supports only artboards. All three apps let you use grids and column layouts inside artboards. Figma's implementation feels more powerful because you can nest.

Adobe Suite

Figma is preferred due to how it handles components and variants. Being able to see all variants in 1 component at the same time and draw interactions between each variant is big within the design space. Also auto layout is top notch when doing responsive work. Lastly Figma has a paid and free tier, as opposed to Adobe.
