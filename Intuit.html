Executive Summary

Summary of Intuits Current Dispostions

Inuit is the premiere Software for the average persons financial needs, specializing in financial software. With 14,200 employees, worldwide. With over 20 offices in 9 countries and $12.7 billion dollars in revenue, Intuit has a diverse portfolio enabling it to serve over 100 million people worldwide. Its services include the tax preparation software TurboTax, the small business accounting software QuickBooks, Mint a personal finance and accountability application and more recently they aquired credit monitoring service known as CreditKarma, and Mailchimp a premiere email marketing program. Inuits products revolve around a heavy marketing strategy with the company spending over $100 million dollars advertising in digital, print, and national tv within the year. They specialize in premium ad units which sees their ads spread across more than 250 media properties. They have also launched/acquired several new products in the last year.

Their strategy targets their primary consumer the small business owner. Their strategy is extensively customer facing and focuses on addressing broader issues customers may be facing. This worked well with their successful Ask the Expert campaign. Which was developed internally and helped customers and Inuit to identify problems solutions and needs to the products in this case specifically QuickBooks.

Competitive Analysis

A Comparative Analysis of Intuit vs its Direct Competitors

Inuits mains competitors exist outside its domestic sphere and our prevalent among global entitys. They primarily have their market shares in foreign countries. This includes Thomas Reuters, Zoho, Equiniti, Nemetchek, sage, Fis, Xero, and Gusto

Xero was a burgeoning competitor at one point. Xero arose a primary competitor in 2010-2011 capturing much of the global market and expanding quickly and taking up market share. However, Intuit countered, by revitalizing QuickBooks Online.

H&R block and Blucora are local competitors. Its biggest competitor is Thomas Rueters.

Of course, another main competitor is public sector and outside accountants.

Competitive Analysis

Competitive Advantage

Their competitive advantage and main advantage is theyre deeply embedded in the workflow of individuals and businesses, making many hesitant to switch. A neutral is that the software typically takes a while to learn, leading to an initial drop-off though this makes customers more hesitant to switch because of time invested and transferring accounting records. The educational program is also a valuable asset as its gets students studying accounting into lifetime users. QuickBooks in particular is the Gold Standard, and in most cases, it’s better for them to use the most understood software in the market. Because people want to use accountants who use QuickBooks and vice versa. Lastly Platform mediated networks and third party tools. Barriers to entry, Barriers to exit.
